Enumerated Powers and Constitutional Responsibility

The Constitution establishes a federal government with specific and limited authority. Article I, Section 8 outlines the responsibilities of Congress and provides the framework for federal action. These powers form the basis of lawful governance and guide how federal programs should operate.

  1. Collect taxes
  2. Spend for national purposes
  3. Borrow money
  4. Coin and regulate money
  5. Regulate interstate and foreign commerce
  6. Establish post offices and roads
  7. Define citizenship
  8. Create uniform bankruptcy laws
  9. Protect patents and copyrights
  10. Create lower federal courts
  11. Define and punish piracy
  12. Declare war
  13. Raise and support armies and navies
  14. Call forth the militia
  15. Make laws necessary and proper to carry out these responsibilities

These powers provide a clear structure for federal action. They also set boundaries. Federal programs should be connected to the responsibilities listed in the Constitution. This approach strengthens accountability and helps prevent the expansion of authority into areas that belong to states, local communities, or individuals.

I support a constitutional approach that respects these limits. A government that follows its defined responsibilities is more predictable, more transparent, and more responsive to the people it serves. This approach encourages careful budgeting, clear priorities, and a stronger focus on national responsibilities such as defense, interstate commerce, and the protection of individual rights.

My goal is to promote policies that reflect this framework. I support legislation that aligns with the powers listed in the Constitution and I support efforts to review federal programs that fall outside this structure. A clear constitutional foundation helps create a government that is stable, efficient, and respectful of the people who grant it authority.

The Constitution provides structure and responsibility for federal power.
A stable government begins with clear limits and consistent application of the law.

A Constitutional Framework for Representation

I consider myself a Constitutionalist. I believe the federal government functions best when it operates within clearly defined limits and responsibilities.

The Constitution establishes a government with specific and enumerated powers. When those limits are respected, government becomes more predictable, more transparent, and more accountable to the people it serves.

The Constitution provides structure and responsibility for federal power.
A stable government begins with clear boundaries and consistent application of the law.

Article I, Section 8

The Constitution outlines the responsibilities of Congress in Article I, Section 8. These powers form the basis of lawful federal action:

  1. Collect taxes
  2. Spend for national purposes
  3. Borrow money
  4. Coin and regulate money
  5. Regulate interstate and foreign commerce
  6. Establish post offices and roads
  7. Define citizenship
  8. Create uniform bankruptcy laws
  9. Protect patents and copyrights
  10. Create lower federal courts
  11. Define and punish piracy
  12. Declare war
  13. Raise and support armies and navies
  14. Call forth the militia
  15. Make laws necessary and proper to carry out these responsibilities

These powers provide both authority and limits. Federal programs should be directly connected to these responsibilities. Areas outside this framework are best handled by states, local communities, or individuals.

Governing with Clear Limits

I support legislation that aligns with the powers granted to Congress and I support efforts to review federal programs that fall outside this structure.

Respecting constitutional limits encourages responsible budgeting, clear priorities, and a stronger focus on national responsibilities such as defense, interstate commerce, and the protection of individual rights.
